## Kongming 영문 백과사전
생몰: AD 203–253
[역사] Zhuge Ke was the son of Zhuge Jin of Wu. He was a nephew to Zhuge Liang. He initially served as a tutor to Sun Deng. In AD 234, Ke sought to be appointed as the official in charge of Danyang. Though Zhuge Ke insisted that he could raise a large army drafted from the people of the region, numerous scholars (including his father Zhuge Jin apparently) doubted his ability. Zhuge Ke was nevertheless appointed to be Prefect of Danyang. In this capacity, Zhuge Ke launched a campaign against the hills people of the area. He forced dissidents into submission and recruited more soldiers into the army as promised. He also collected grain.*
