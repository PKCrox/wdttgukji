Liu Biao, courtesy name Jingsheng, was a Chinese military general, politician, and warlord who lived in the late Eastern Han dynasty of China. He is best known for serving as the governor of Jing Province from 192 until his death in 208. [역사] Liu Biao was born in AD 142. He was tall, smart, and handsome. In AD 190, Dong Zhuo appointed Liu Biao to be Inspector of Jing province. Thanks to the advice of Kuai Yue and Kuai Liang, Liu Biao established his authority in Jing. The following year, Sun Jian attacked Liu Biao, but was struck by an arrow and died. For several years afterward, Liu Biao consolidated his power within the region.

Liu Biao turned Jing province into a haven for scholars and philosophers. He allowed Song Zhong to establish an academy for teaching the classics. From all over the land, a multitude of men of learning came to study in Liu Biao’s territory. Militarily, Liu Biao and his ally Zhang Xiu (mostly the latter) occasionally clashed with Cao Cao.
